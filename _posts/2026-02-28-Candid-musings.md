Life used to be simple back a decade, Things used to be simple, no autocompletes.  Just us and our experiments with mistakes and learning. Life evolved too fast.


## Rant from a runt

This is my first post, Since I wanted to build my blog writing skills, I thought of taking several technical topics, such as how to setup zed editor for competitive coding, how I built this blog using antigravity in a day, and how the new delta lake architecture and iceberg architecture impacts the engineering world. But as I started typing the words and with each word being constantly autocompleted, I felt my post is going to be yet another competition of whether autocomplete can guess my thoughts. The urge to just write freely got the better of me, so I'm starting with a raw post from my thoughts rather than a well-structured one. 


Well, Life used to be simple before the advent of AI. Now, everything is growing and changing at a wild pace. It is simply astounding to think of the numerous things coding can solve. With AlphaFold solving the mysteries of biology in a fraction of the time it took humans to find answers for decades.  Things are pretty clear; many mysteries of life are going to be solved. Trying to figure out where coding is headed or where the job market is headed is a panic-causing thought. But to every thought that stems from fear, there is a thought that lies in the opposite side, which instills hope. I heard a recent podcast by danny koe where, amidst people having fears of losing jobs, he was talking about mentality of a person who thrives not lives. The world is not perfect by any yard stick. He talks about world having lot of problems. But to see this in a different light, it is solving these problems that makes money for a person. So as long as there are problems, there will be a way to make money and therefore there will be a plethora of jobs. I used to think that as a software engineer, things were pretty straightforward. Turns out our life had a curve ball waiting for us. More than the yin-yang of evil fighting goodness and evening things out. Nowadays, it is just a battle of pessimism fighting optimism with every news in tech space.


During such said news and whenever I am on a downward spiral as a simpleton, I think, as long as I keep walking and do the best I can, I don't think I will regret the choices I make. But at every turn, there is a part of work or some fraction of life that I cannot give my everything to. I don't know if my feelings will change in the future. But to calm my nerves, I say to myself, and I genuinely believe, among all the good things and bad things I have experienced, I can pretty much say I am blessed to be wherever I am right now. To think about this fluctuation in optimism is also fascinating. Now a days people are  motivated or postively pushed by a number of different ways. Everyone group's belief is fragmented. No major groups think alike anymore. One may follow stoicism to accept things as it is and move ahead with life. Some may choose to work on the past events and go for therapy to fix the things. Some just rawdog life on the trust in themselves. Due to all the these I start believing we are heading in a better direction with respect to mental health. But then the rate of mental fragility is also going up. Hence, balancing all the things that came before. 



My entire life, I have been listening to motivational talks, and now, instead of them, I am going to work with the law of large numbers and discipline to better myself. I am going to try to post some content on a weekly basis. Let us see if this fetches me anything. Let us hopefully meet in the next post. Toodles!
